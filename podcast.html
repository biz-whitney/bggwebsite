False Choices & Poisoned Futures: Examining Environmental Racism

I interview Black Millennials 4 Flint's Founder, Chief Executive Officer, and President LaTricea D. Adams and Sierra Club's community organizer Justin Onwenu. Together, we define environmental racism and sacrifice zones; discuss their successful coalition practices and how to they take action for their communities; the realities of receiving funding and amplifying their narratives; and how to cultivate joy in a continuously daunting world.

The Violence of Silence: Racial Equality and The Outdoors

Following the events targeting a black man like Christian cooper, we are continuing to learn the outdoors are not safe for everyone. What does racial equity within the outdoors mean given the current state of the world? As more BIPOC face microaggressions in natural spaces, how will organizations and communities step up to fight back against bigotry to prioritize a clean, healthy, & safe environment for all?

Talking Circular Economy: Making Synthetic Hair More Sustainable

We are joined with Ciara Imani May, founder of Rebundle, a company closing the loop on synthetic hair waste one bundle at a time. We will be talking about what it means to generate a circular economy, how to make an environmentally friendly and culturally conscious product, and how to support young entrepreneurs of color working to save the world.

Put Your Money Where Your Values Are: Personal Finance & The Planet

Given this fiscally-uncertain world for the 99%, how can we put our money where our values are? This segment will feature an interview with Joelle Sostheim to learn how to use personal finance as a tool to make alternative, environmentally-conscious spending, and investments. As well as Lila Holzman, from As You Sow, tells us how we can place our accrued wealth into environmentally-friendly funding sources in order to invest in companies that are doing good, rather than bad, things to the planet.

Climate Change & Disabilities: How'd we get here?

Kristy interviews Marsha Saxton and Alex Ghenis from the World Institute on Disabilities (WID) about climate change & disabilities. Given the global pandemic, Kristy takes the intersection of disability justice and climate change to find its connection to coronavirus. Without the infrastructure necessary to support people with disabilities, communities remain disappointingly unfit to support this group. Marsha and Alex, nonetheless, share their optimism and practical solutions in hopes to build a more inclusive, adaptable climate-changing-world.

Self-Care In The Time of Climate Crisis

It is expected that the climate crisis has and will continue to have a detrimental impact on public health, particularly on society’s mental health. As the news likes to convince us that the world is falling apart (and in some ways it is), how are we going to take care of ourselves and each other? Listen to this episode with Brittney Enin, the Self Care Consultant.

Save The Planet, Save Your Money

In our current climate crisis, how can we reach the masses through storytelling, connection, and education? In this episode of Brown Girl Green, I meet with Environmental “Eco-Influencer” Go Green Save Green, Ariel Maldonado. Ariel uses Instagram to engage her audience about how to live sustainably and to save money along the way.

Pilot Episode

In the pilot episode of Brown Girl Green, Kristy, a Filipina American environmental activist discusses the origins of Brown Girl Green. From activist burnout to trying to find diplomacy (or love?) on tinder, this episode lays the groundwork of what you can expect to watch in Season 1.
